Dataset Description¶

"The dataset contains 1,000,000 playlists, including playlist titles and track titles, created by users on the Spotify platform between January 2010 and October 2017. The evaluation task is automatic playlist continuation: given a seed playlist title and/or initial set of tracks in a playlist, to predict the subsequent tracks in that playlist. This is an open-ended challenge intended to encourage research in music recommendations, and no prizes will be awarded (other than bragging rights)."

After the Vietnam War, there were large counts of Incarcerated Veterans.



There were also a significant amount of diagnosed mental disorders (2011-2012).
The Vietnam War was also known as the “Working Class War”. 80 percent of the 2.5 million enlisted men who served in Vietnam came from working class and impoverished backgrounds.
Springsteen is referring to being in financial trouble and is sent to fight in the war.
Vietnam veterans – even today – struggle with finding employment after the Vietnam War.
Despite the title and the tune, Springsteen warns us right off the bat: this isn’t going to be a happy, simple, patriotic song.

He’s not just born in a dead man’s town, but born down in a dead man’s town. He is kicked all his life, from the very first day. The beating takes its toll, and he spends his life accommodating his wounds. The despair is absolute. The transition to the fist-bumping chorus is unmistakably ironic.

“Born down in a dead man’s town” suggests the protagonist is A. Born in a town where not very much happens or B. He is born in a town that is very dangerous to live in.
“Long Gone Daddy” is a reference to the eminent Hank Williams, a representation of the tragic Americana evoked by Springsteen’s anthem

The phrase “Cool rocking Daddy” is bitterly sarcastic: Unlike the global rock star singing the song, the protagonist is unknown and forgotten by his country.
According to self-reporting of inmates, at one point a quarter of the American prison population consisted of vets. Further studies showed that the number was vastly inflated, but the fact remains that a lot of Vietnam veterans spent time in prison that probably wouldn’t have had they not been in the war.

The “refinery” may be an allusion to the Bayway Refinery and East Jersey State Prison in Elizabeth/Linden and Rahway, NJ, respectively. The two spots are only about 15 minutes apart, not far from Newark.
Saigon (now called Ho Chi Minh City) was the capital of South Vietnam (the half that Americans fought for).

It was common for soldiers to fall for local women during their years in Vietnam, and these sometimes happy but often tragic love stories have inspired a lot of music.
Veterans had trouble not only finding jobs, but also finding adequate support from the V.A. (Veterans Affairs) and the government in general
The picture is the only memento he has left.

The fact that he is in her arms, reversing the stereotypical image of the little lady in the big soldier’s arms, hints that this isn’t a photo of the couple during happier times, but a photo of the lover mourning over her soldier’s corpse. It’s a bellic, American pietà.
The end of this song features a long coda, but the engineer on that session, Toby Scott, recalled that it was originally even longer:

 I’d gotten a good cue mix for everybody, so they all could hear it, and the takes were like 10 minutes long. On the final cut there’s a rather long ride-out, but it was even longer and we cut it down by three or four minutes. The band just kept playing. There were eight takes of the song, take six was the master — thanks to Max perfecting his bass-drum pattern — and when the band members came into the control room after the first couple of takes and heard the track, they too were going, ‘My God, we’ve never heard anything like this before!’ It was totally, revolutionarily different-sounding to anything else at that time.
